Most event software stops at registration. ClearPath keeps going — to the vendor map, the volunteer mobile sign-in, the security gate, the sponsor payment tracker. Every person involved in your event sees a surface that respects their time.

Beyond the gate

The depth nobody else builds.

Security gate
Scan QR codes or search by name.

Every ticket carries a QR. Every gate volunteer scans them on a phone. No paper rosters, no "is this person on the list?" lookup loops. Manual fallback by name when phones die.

Volunteer mobile
Volunteers sign in by QR.

Print one QR code, hand it out at the briefing. Volunteers scan it, enter a PIN and their name, and they're in the event-day app — assignment, instructions, contacts. Kill all sessions in one click when the show closes.

Multi-stage entertainment
Bands schedule themselves.

Add a stage, add a band, set genres and set times. Bands invited via email, schedule auto-publishes to the public site. Active toggle so cancellations don't strand attendees with stale info.

In the field
Cruisin' Downtown.
25 years independent · $80K raised every year.

Manchester Rotary Club has been running Cruisin' Downtown for twenty-five years. Eighty thousand dollars raised every year for local children's and family charities — every dollar routed through registration nights, vendor placement, sponsor checks, t-shirt sales, donations, the volunteer schedule, the security gate.

Year by year, the operational complexity got further ahead of the spreadsheets. A managed-services firm would charge tens of thousands a year to administer an event at this scale. The Rotary Club declined.

ClearPath put it all on one panel. Same volunteer team. Same hours. More time on the parts of the show that actually matter — the cars, the music, the people walking the strip on a long Saturday afternoon.

Operator questions

The objections we hear.
Answered straight.

01
We already use Eventbrite / a custom site / spreadsheets. What's the cost of switching?

Nothing structural. ClearPath imports prior years' rosters, vendor lists, and sponsor histories. Your first event is the migration — not a parallel run. If we can't make the switch boring, we'll tell you on the first call.

02
What happens on event day if your platform goes down?

Two answers. First, the platform is read-mostly on event day and cached on every device — gate scanning and lookup keep working through brief network blips. Second, every screen has a manual fallback (search by name, paper roster export) so a worst-case outage doesn't strand the gate.

03
Do we own our data?

Yes. Registrants, vendors, sponsors, volunteers — all exportable as CSV any time, no friction. We don't sell aggregated event data. We don't train models on it. If you leave, you leave with everything.

04
Can vendors and sponsors apply without creating ClearPath accounts?

Yes. Public application forms live on your event's domain. Vendors fill them out, pay through Stripe, get a confirmation email. They never see "ClearPath" until you place them on the map. The platform runs quietly behind your event's brand.

05
Will my volunteer team actually use it?

The volunteer signup is a public web page — no app install, no login. The event-day mobile portal is QR-and-PIN access on whatever phone they brought. We've watched seventy-year-old Rotarians sign in faster than the kid running the merch table. If your volunteers can scan a parking-meter QR, they can use this.
