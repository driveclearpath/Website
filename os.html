What it does

Diagnose your shop the way
the corporations diagnosing it for acquisition do.

The roll-ups have a playbook. They walk into every independent shop in your zip code and read the same six metrics. They know exactly which shops to buy and which to walk past. ClearPath OS gives you their playbook — pointed at fixing your shop instead of pricing it.

01

KPIs that read like a corporate playbook.

ARO, GP%, hours billed, comeback rate, declined-work value, technician utilization, parts margin, AOV, ticket count by service category — the same sixty-five metrics the chains use to evaluate every independent shop they're considering. Not a dashboard. A diagnosis.

02

Twelve diagnostic engines, not dashboards.

Most shop dashboards show you a number and stop. ClearPath's engines show you why the number is what it is — what changed, who drove it, what to do today. When ARO drops six dollars, you don't get a chart. You get a list of the three causes and the one tech driving them.

04

Coaching at the service writer.

ClearPath reads your live RO, your customer's history, and your shop's KPI patterns — then suggests the upsell, the bundle, the warning to flag. Not a script. A second pair of eyes that already knows your numbers and the customer in front of you. Service writers stop guessing and start closing.

06

Your data trains nothing.

Your customer histories, your tech performance, your margins — none of it goes into training, none of it gets aggregated and sold. Your shop's data stays your shop's data. We can put that in writing.

A note from the founder

There's a reason small auto shops keep getting bought up by corporate America.

It isn't that they're bad shops. It's that they don't have the operational sophistication to compete with Caliber, Driven Brands, Christian Brothers — the chains scanning every independent shop in their zip code for the next acquisition. The roll-up wins because the family-owned shop can't see what the corporate playbook sees: the leaks, the patterns, the customers quietly drifting.

I built ClearPath OS because I owned a shop and couldn't always be at the shop. I needed the same operational layer those firms use to evaluate which shop to buy next, but pointed at running mine instead — so I could see what was leaking, what was leaving, what to fix before it cost me.

That's still what it does. Every shop gets the corporate diagnosis without the corporate consequences. You see what they'd see. You fix what they'd find. You keep your shop.

You stay independent. You stay family-owned. You run your business like a corporation — and corporate America never gets the chance to roll you up.

That's the whole point.

Shop owner questions

The objections we hear.
Answered straight.

01
We use Tekmetric. What does ClearPath replace?

Nothing. ClearPath reads from your authorized Tekmetric API connection and lives alongside it. Your service writers keep using the same screen they're using today. The diagnosis surfaces wherever your team already looks — email, screen, SMS overlay. Migration is zero hours of bay downtime because there's nothing to migrate.

02
We're on Mitchell, Shop-Ware, or ProDemand. Can we use ClearPath?

Not today, but those integrations are on the roadmap. We add SMS systems one at a time and certify each one before we claim support — no marketing checklists for integrations we haven't built. Tell us what you're on and we'll tell you our timeline. Early-pilot slots open up as each integration is certified.

03
How accurate are the KPIs?

As accurate as your SMS data. ClearPath doesn't invent numbers — it reads them, aggregates them with the rules the chains use, and shows you the same picture they'd see. If your data has gaps, ClearPath flags them. If your data is clean, the diagnoses are.

04
How long is training?

For owners and managers, less than an hour. For service writers, the system surfaces inside the workflow they already use — there's nothing new to learn beyond "look at this when the badge is red." We built it for shop floors, not classrooms.

05
Will my service writers actually use it?

If a service writer can read the SMS screen during a customer conversation, they can use ClearPath. The recommendation appears next to the customer record they're already looking at. No app, no login, no second tab. Adoption is the workflow.

06
What about my customer data — does it train AI?

No. Section 18 of the Tekmetric API license explicitly forbids using shop data to train models. We treat that as the floor across every integration. Your data isn't used to train, isn't sold in aggregate, isn't shared with other shops. We sign that into the contract.
